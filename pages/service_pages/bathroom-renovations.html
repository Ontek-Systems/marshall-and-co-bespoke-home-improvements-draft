A Bathroom That Works Beautifully

Bathrooms demand precision. The waterproofing has to be right behind every tile, the plumbing first fix has to be placed accurately, and the tiling has to be set out in a way that works with the room instead of just the wall in isolation. Lee has done this enough times to know exactly where corners get cut and how to avoid every one of them.

A bathroom renovation with Marshall & Co. starts with a full strip-out to bare substrate. What goes back in is entirely your brief: walk-in shower, freestanding bath, heated towel rails, underfloor heating, bespoke vanity unit. Lee sources quality sanitaryware and fixtures and installs them to a standard that lasts.

No trades are brought in from outside. Lee manages every aspect of the fit-out, which means there's one person accountable from first fix to final decoration.

Waterproofing You Can Trust

Behind every tile in a wet area, the substrate needs to be waterproofed properly. Lee uses tanking systems applied to the correct standard, rather than a quick brush-on that fails within three years.

Tiling Set Out Properly

Good tiling starts with good set-out: working from the focal points, keeping cuts symmetrical, leaving consistent joints. Lee plans the layout before the first tile goes on, not while he's halfway through.

One Trade, Full Scope
